The Venue

The Ole Hanson Beach Club, a designated historic landmark in San Clemente, California, was completed in 1928 as part of city founder Ole Hanson’s vision for a “Spanish Village by the Sea.” Designed in the Spanish Colonial Revival style, the club features arched walkways, ornate tile work, and sweeping ocean views, quickly becoming a hub for recreation and community life. Over the decades, it has evolved from a public swimming facility to a cherished venue for weddings and special events, and in 2016 it was officially renamed the Ole Hanson Beach Club to honor its historical significance. Today, it stands as a celebrated symbol of San Clemente’s rich architectural heritage and coastal charm.
